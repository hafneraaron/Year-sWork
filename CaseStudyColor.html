The header text is orange because orange is associated with citrus, and fall, making the webpage smell like flowers, citrus, and cinnamon. Orange also catches the eye easily, which makes it good for accents and headers. In this webpage, I will use it for both. (The outlines are orange.) The header's background color is a lighter brown than the rest of the page because I wanted to visually differentiate the two sections. I believe differentiating the sections creates a sense of order and clarity of purpose. I added outlines for the same reason.

I decided to make the background of the website a dark, low-saturation brown because it is associated with nature, masculinity, and simplicit. This conveys a appreciation of nature and being made by hand. Brown is also associated with the fall, which, coupled with the orange accents around the page, satisfies the client's wish for the webpage to smell like flowers, citrus, and cinnamon I made the paragraph text a bright green because it sticks out from the background, and because green is also associated with nature. According to Forbes, light green is also a very accepting color, which means that it gives off a sense of appreciation for other cultures. This therefor satisfies the client's specification for the webpage: eclectic mix of world music
